About Me

Hello there, my name is Jesse! I'm a Computer Science major at Montana State University planning to graduate in May 2025. I'm currently doing NASA funded research at MSU's BOREALIS lab. My project is KOABC, which aims to introduce K-12 students to arduino coding through a block code website.

I love hiking, baking, and playing video / board games. Many years of playing flash games, trying free apps, and admiring websites have led me to an interest in software engineering. From this background, I have learned that importance of human-centered design, which is the central pillar of my design philosophy.

Growing Up in Montana

I've lived in Montana for the majority of my life, and it's brought many unique opportunities and challenges. For most of my peers and myself, we had limited computer access outside of school. This restriction fostered passion in web and cloud development for me, to allow the greatest accessibility to my applications possible.

Experience

Work

Alongside my current work at the BOREALIS lab, I also worked as a teaching aid for a Helena College computer science summer camp in the summer of 2022. This job centered around teaching middle school students about computer science fundamentals: explaining how to code in python, navigate linux terminals, and cybersecurity fundamentals. This work required detailed attention and explanation to students to ensure their understanding. From this experience, I also learned more about each of the topics taught: gaining insight on how to explain concepts without jargin and draw parallels to common ideas.

During the summer of 2024, I worked at Costco Wholesale as a frontend employee. This work required frequent coordination and communcation with coworkers to ensure customer satisfaction. Throughout the summer, I performed many different roles throughout the warehouse: assisting cashiers, running carts, fixing product presentation, checking customer cards, and making food court orders. The breadth of this work taught me to be flexible and continually keep learning new things. This is the same as in software engineering, since new technologies are constantly being created and updated over time. In addition, the most well rounded developers can serve multiple roles on the same project.

Congressional App Challenge

While I was in highschool, I decided to make an application to teach drivers safety to highschoolers for the Congressional App Challenge. This was because while teenagers take drivers education courses, there are many difficult situations that happen on the road that aren't covered in drivers ed. In addition, there are dangerous behaviours like using a phone while driving that are common among teens. My app was focused on teaching about these aspects, working as suplemental material to drivers education courses. After making the application, I submitted it to the competition and ended up being the 2021 Congressional App Challenge winner for Montana's senatorial district.
